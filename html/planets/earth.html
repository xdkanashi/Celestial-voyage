Earth is the third planet from the Sun in our solar system and the only known celestial body to support life. It is the fifth-largest planet in our solar system and is often referred to as the "Blue Planet" due to the abundance of water on its surface, giving it a distinct blue appearance when viewed from space.

Earth's surface is approximately 71% water and 29% land. The highest point on Earth is the summit of Mount Everest, standing at 29,029 feet (8,848 meters) above sea level. Conversely, the lowest point on land is the shoreline of the Dead Sea, located about 1,410 feet (430 meters) below sea level. Earth is also the only known place in the universe where life has been discovered, making it precious planet.
